Guide to Tenants Rights

FAIR HOUSING IS A RIGHT!
The Pennsylvania Human Relations Act prohibits discrimination in all housing transactions including but not limited to sales, rental, finance, providing reasonable accommodations to housing or commercial properties based on race, color, national origin, religion, sex, familial status, disability, age (over 40 in housing), ancestry, or pregnancy.

What are my rights as a tenant?

Landlords cannot require more than two months’ rent as a security deposit.

Landlords must return the security deposit within 30 days of termination of the lease.

If the landlord keeps part of the security deposit, they must provid a written list of damages and the remaining balance within 30 days. (Unpaid rent may also be deducted from the security deposit.)

Landlords must give tenants 10 days to pay rent before filing for eviction.

Landlords cannot evict a tenant without going through the court process.

Tenants are allowed to participate in a tenants’ union or association without being evicted or denied lease renewal.

Legal Guarantees

Tenants have a legal right to rental housing that meets basic structural, health and safety standards. This is called the “implied warranty of habitability.” Under this court doctrine, a landlord is responsible to repair functional problems, including…

  • inadequate heat
  • leaking roof
  • defective plumbing
  • lack of hot water
  • leaking toilet and pipes
  • broken locks on entrance doors
  • hazardous steps
  • broken floors
  • dangerous wiring
  • infestation by pests

A BREACH OF THE IMPLIED WARRANTY also may arise in the event of the following conditions: improper ventilation of a gas hot water heater or gas space heater, falling plaster, defective electrical wiring, a malfunctioning water system, defective windows, and broken porch steps and railings.

If the landlord fails to make repairs,

A TENANT CAN:

  1. Document the repair issues, take pictures, and get witnesses.
  2. Make a repair request to your landlord in writing. Send an email or a letter by certified mail. Keep copies.
  3. Withhold rent if there are serious repair issues. Send another letter to your landlord stating your intention to withhold rent unless they make repairs. Escrow your rent in specified account to deposit your rent until repairs are made.

A landlord is not required to repair damages caused by the tenant. Also, tenants bear the burden of proof, which means they need to prove they have notified the landlord of the problem and given them reasonable time to make the necessary repairs. This is why documentation is important.
